The Realisation

When I started working as a manager, I came to realise I had more to offer in the New Zealand workforce and that I do not fit well into the industry I was in. ( I came to realise that if I was the spend my time and dedicate myself to an industry, I would rather immerse myself into technology, a long time passion of mine , that I was passionate about since I was young ) I am more passionate about technology and how things come together. I have spent most of my free time learning about programming languages and how AI, ML, and advances in modern computing have influenced our daily lives as individuals and corporations. I thought to myself, “Hey, why don’t I learn more about this and start working in the tech industry. I know I will fit perfectly into the industry. After all, this will not be just a career. It is me combining my passion projects and converting it into a career.” So now comes the new beginning.

The New Beginning

In 2017 I had a plan. I had the burning desire to go back to studies. So, I had a chat with my manager. He was very understanding, and I got his full support. I handed in my 8 weeks’ notice and enrolled at the University of Auckland to do a Bachelors’ degree in Computer Science and Information Systems. I haven’t looked back since.

Living independently taught me many things, and these lessons helped me construct My Values.
